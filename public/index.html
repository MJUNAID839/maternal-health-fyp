Feature Importance

Model Analysis
Model Feature Contributions
Pulse Rate

Most significant contributor (45%). Abnormal heart rate (below 60 or above 100 bpm) significantly increases risk.

Oxygen Saturation (SpO2)

Second most important (35%). Values below 95% indicate potential respiratory issues.

Temperature

Contributes 20% to risk assessment. Abnormal temperatures (below 36°C or above 37.5°C) increase risk.

How Feature Importance Works

Our XGBoost model analyzes each vital sign and calculates its contribution to the final risk score. Feature importance is determined by how much each feature reduces prediction error across all decision trees.

  • Pulse Rate: Most critical - sudden changes indicate cardiovascular stress
  • SpO2: Essential for detecting oxygen deprivation in mother and fetus
  • Temperature: Key indicator of infection or inflammatory response
